What is gastroenteritis?

Gastroenteritis is an infection of the stomach and bowel. When people say they have a "stomach bug" they usually mean gastroenteritis. Most of the time gastroenteritis is caused by a virus but bacteria and parasites can also cause it. The infection causes runny or watery bowel motions (diarrhea) and sometimes vomiting. It is very easy to spread to other people.

What is this study about?

The GI IMPACT Study seeks to understand how healthcare providers diagnose (find out why someone is sick) and treat gastrointestinal infections. There is a new test that can be used to look at stool specimens for 22 different kinds of bacteria, viruses, and parasites that can cause diarrhea and vomiting. This test is called the FilmArray GI Panel and additional information about it can be found by clicking here.
